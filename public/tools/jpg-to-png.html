Why convert JPG to PNG?

JPG is great for photos, but PNG is preferred for crisp graphics, UI elements, and logos. PNG uses lossless compression and supports transparency, which makes it a better fit when you need clean edges or want an image to sit on top of a colored or textured background.

Converting JPG to PNG with GhostConvert is useful when you:

  • Prepare logo files or icons for websites and apps.
  • Need graphics with transparent backgrounds for presentations or thumbnails.
  • Work with UI assets that should stay sharp at different sizes.
  • Want to avoid further quality loss from repeated JPG saves.

The output PNG files are ready to drop into design tools, CMS platforms, and code projects without extra cleanup.

Tips for best results

  • Start from the highest-quality JPG you have to keep edges and text crisp.
  • If you need true transparency, consider removing the background in an editor after converting.
  • Use PNG for logos, icons, buttons, and any element that overlays other colors or images.

Frequently asked questions

Will this add transparency to my image?

Converting from JPG to PNG does not automatically remove backgrounds. It creates a PNG version of your image, which can support transparency if you edit it later in a graphics tool.

Is PNG higher quality than JPG?

PNG is lossless, meaning it doesn’t introduce new compression artifacts when saved. Your converted PNG will look as good as the source JPG, and future saves will not degrade quality the way repeated JPG saves can.
