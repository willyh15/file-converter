Why delete pages from a PDF?

Many PDFs include pages that are unnecessary: blank backs, scanned covers, pages meant for internal use, or personal information you don’t want to send. Removing pages makes the document cleaner and smaller.

Typical reasons to delete PDF pages:

  • Remove blank pages from a scan.
  • Delete title covers before sending a report.
  • Remove pages containing sensitive personal data.
  • Trim unnecessary appendix pages.
  • Shorten a document to meet upload limits.

GhostConvert makes it easy: upload a file, type the pages to remove, and download the new PDF.

How page deletion works

  1. You upload a PDF from your device.
  2. Enter the pages you want removed.
  3. The pages are removed on the server.
  4. You download a clean PDF without those pages.

The original file isn’t changed — a new PDF is created containing only the pages you want.

Best uses

  • Submitting assignments without cover pages.
  • Sending only the signed pages of a contract.
  • Splitting a large document into sections.
  • Cleaning scanned PDFs of irrelevant pages.

Frequently asked questions

Does this change page content?

No — GhostConvert only removes pages. The remaining pages stay exactly the same.

Can I delete multiple ranges?

Yes — you can use a combination of single pages and dash ranges like: 2,5-8,11.

What happens to my file?

Files are stored only long enough to create the output file and are deleted shortly after as described in the Privacy Policy.

Files are processed securely and automatically deleted after a short period.
