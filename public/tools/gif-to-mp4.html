Why convert GIF to MP4?

Animated GIFs are easy to share but they were never designed for modern video. They use older compression, have limited colors, and can quickly grow to several megabytes even for short loops. MP4 uses modern video codecs that store the same animation much more efficiently while keeping it smooth and sharp.

Converting a GIF to MP4 with GhostConvert typically gives:

  • Smaller file sizes, which means faster uploads and less storage.
  • Smoother playback on phones, laptops, and social apps.
  • Better compatibility with video editors and scheduling tools.

Once converted, you can post the MP4 to platforms that prefer video over GIFs, or use it inside presentations and editing workflows without bloated file sizes.

Frequently asked questions

Will the MP4 still loop?

MP4 files do not loop by themselves, but most social platforms and video players give you an option to repeat the clip. You can also place the MP4 on a timeline in your editor and loop it as many times as you like.

Can I keep the transparent background?

Standard MP4 does not support transparency. If your GIF uses transparent areas, the converted MP4 will have a solid background instead (as defined by the encoder). For overlays with transparency you may want to keep a PNG sequence as well.
