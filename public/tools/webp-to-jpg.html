Why convert WEBP to JPG?

WEBP is great for the modern web, but many older editors, apps, and devices still only understand JPG. Converting WEBP to JPG lets you reuse downloaded images in places that don’t yet support newer formats.

  • Open WEBP images in legacy photo editors
  • Upload images to older CMSs, forums, or marketplace tools
  • Share images with people using older phones and computers
  • Export screenshots or graphics in a format any app can read

How WEBP → JPG conversion works

Frequently asked questions

Will the JPG be larger than the WEBP?

In many cases, yes — WEBP is usually more efficient than JPG. The goal here is compatibility: JPG trades a bit of extra file size for support in older tools.

What happens to transparency?

JPG does not support transparency. Transparent areas in your WEBP image will be rendered against a solid background (often white) in the converted JPG.
